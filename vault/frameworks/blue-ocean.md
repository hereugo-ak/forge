# Blue Ocean Strategy

## Purpose
Identify whether the company can create **uncontested market space** through value innovation — simultaneously pursuing differentiation and low cost.

## The Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create (ERRC) Grid

| Action | What | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Eliminate** | [What factors that the industry takes for granted should be eliminated?] | Remove elements that don't add value |
| **Reduce** | [What factors should be reduced well below the industry standard?] | Over-delivery in areas that don't drive buyer value |
| **Raise** | [What factors should be raised well above the industry standard?] | Under-delivery in areas that drive buyer value |
| **Create** | [What factors should be created that the industry has never offered?] | New sources of value |

## Strategy Canvas
Plot the company vs. competitors on key competitive factors:
- X-axis: Competitive factors (price, quality, features, service, etc.)
- Y-axis: Offering level (low to high)
- Compare the shape of the value curve

## When to Use
- Market creation / category design
- Escaping competitive traps (red oceans)
- Differentiation strategy
- **NOT** for optimizing within an existing market structure

## HYPERION Application
The Strategy Analyst (Agent 14) uses Blue Ocean when the question is about creating new markets or escaping commoditization. It builds the strategy canvas comparing the company to competitors and identifies the white space in the value curve.
