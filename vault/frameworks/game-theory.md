# Game Theory for Competitive Strategy

## Purpose
Analyze competitive interactions where outcomes depend on the actions of multiple players. Identify dominant strategies and Nash equilibria.

## Key Concepts

### Prisoner's Dilemma
- Both players better off cooperating, but individual incentive to defect
- Application: Price wars, advertising escalation, R&D races
- **Key insight**: If the game is repeated, cooperation can emerge (tit-for-tat)

### Sequential Games
- Players move in sequence (Stackelberg competition)
- First-mover advantage or disadvantage depends on the game
- **Key insight**: Credible commitments change the game

### Signaling
- Actions that reveal private information
- High-cost signals are credible (spending $50M on a factory signals commitment)
- Low-cost signals are not (announcing "we're committed" signals nothing)
- **Key insight**: Signal credibility is proportional to cost
