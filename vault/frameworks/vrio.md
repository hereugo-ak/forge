# VRIO Framework

## Purpose
Evaluate resources and capabilities to identify which provide **sustainable competitive advantage**. Resource-based view (RBV) framework.

## The Four Questions

### V — Value
Does the resource enable the firm to exploit an opportunity or neutralize a threat?
- **Yes** → continue to Rarity
- **No** → Competitive Disadvantage

### R — Rarity
Is the resource currently controlled by only a small number of competing firms?
- **Yes** → continue to Imitability
- **No** → Competitive Parity (temporary advantage)

### I — Imitability
Do firms without the resource face a cost disadvantage in obtaining or developing it?
- **Yes** → continue to Organization
- **No** → Temporary Competitive Advantage

### O — Organization
Are the firm's policies and processes organized to support the exploitation of the valuable, rare, and hard-to-imitate resource?
- **Yes** → Sustained Competitive Advantage
- **No** → Unused Advantage (wealth but no competitive advantage)

## Scoring Matrix

| Resource | Valuable? | Rare? | Imitable? | Organized? | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Resource 1] | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | Disadvantage/Parity/Temporary/Sustained |
| [Resource 2] | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N | Disadvantage/Parity/Temporary/Sustained |

## When to Use
- Resource-based strategy analysis
- Identifying core competencies
- Assessing defensibility of competitive position
- **NOT** for industry-level analysis (use Porter's instead)

## HYPERION Application
The Strategy Analyst (Agent 14) uses VRIO when the question is about what makes a company's position defensible. It identifies the 2-3 resources that provide sustained advantage and assesses whether they're transferable to new markets.
