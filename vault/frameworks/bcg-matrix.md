# BCG Growth-Share Matrix

## Purpose
Plot products/business units on a 2×2 matrix to identify resource allocation priorities.

## The Four Quadrants

### Stars (High Growth, High Share)
- Leaders in high-growth markets
- Generate significant revenue but consume cash to maintain growth
- **Strategy**: Invest to maintain position; will become Cash Cows as growth slows

### Cash Cows (Low Growth, High Share)
- Leaders in low-growth/mature markets
- Generate more cash than they consume
- **Strategy**: Milk for cash; use to fund Stars and Question Marks

### Question Marks (High Growth, Low Share)
- In attractive high-growth markets but low share
- Consume cash; may become Stars or fail
- **Strategy**: Invest selectively in those with path to leadership; divest others

### Dogs (Low Growth, Low Share)
- Weak position in unattractive markets
- Barely break even; consume management attention
- **Strategy**: Divest or harvest for remaining cash

## Matrix Template

| Business Unit | Market Growth Rate | Relative Market Share | Quadrant | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Unit 1] | X% | X.X | Star/Cash Cow/Question Mark/Dog | Invest/Milk/Selective/Divest |
| [Unit 2] | X% | X.X | Star/Cash Cow/Question Mark/Dog | Invest/Milk/Selective/Divest |

## HYPERION Application
The Strategy Analyst (Agent 14, STRONG tier) uses the BCG matrix for portfolio analysis questions. It always pairs the matrix with resource allocation recommendations — a matrix without action is just a chart.
