# ESG Assessment Frameworks

## Purpose
Score companies/strategies on the relevant ESG framework and calculate carbon footprint across Scope 1/2/3.

## ESG Frameworks

### MSCI ESG Ratings
- Industry-relative rating (AAA-CCC)
- 37 ESG key issues across 3 pillars (E, S, G)
- Best for: Investor-facing ESG communication

### SASB Standards
- Industry-specific materiality standards
- 77 industries with distinct ESG metrics
- Best for: Financial reporting integration

### TCFD Recommendations
- Climate-related financial disclosures
- Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics & Targets
- Best for: Investor climate risk assessment

### GRI Standards
- Universal + topic-specific standards
- Stakeholder-centric reporting
- Best for: Multi-stakeholder communication (customers, employees, communities)

### CSRD (EU)
- Mandatory EU sustainability reporting
- Double materiality (impact + financial)
- Best for: EU regulatory compliance

## Carbon Footprint

### Scope 1 — Direct Emissions
- Owned/controlled sources
- Company vehicles, on-site combustion, fugitive emissions

### Scope 2 — Indirect Energy
- Purchased electricity, heat, steam
- Location-based or market-based method

### Scope 3 — Value Chain
- 15 categories (purchased goods, capital goods, transportation, use of sold products, etc.)
- Typically 70-90% of total footprint

## HYPERION Application
The Sustainability Analyst (Agent 10, STANDARD tier) selects the framework that matters for the specific stakeholder (investors want TCFD, regulators want CSRD, customers want GRI). It doesn't just calculate a carbon number — it identifies the specific emission sources that account for 80% of the footprint and calculates the abatement cost for each.
