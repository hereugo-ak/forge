# Porter's Five Forces

## Purpose
Analyze industry attractiveness through five competitive forces. Score each force as **strong**, **moderate**, or **weak**.

## The Five Forces

### 1. Threat of New Entrants
- Capital requirements
- Economies of scale
- Brand identity / switching costs
- Access to distribution channels
- Regulatory barriers
- Absolute cost advantages (proprietary tech, learning curve)

### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers
- Supplier concentration
- Switching costs for suppliers
- Availability of substitute inputs
- Differentiation of inputs
- Forward integration threat

### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers
- Buyer concentration
- Switching costs for buyers
- Price sensitivity
- Threat of backward integration
- Product differentiation

### 4. Threat of Substitutes
- Price-performance tradeoff of substitutes
- Switching costs
- Buyer propensity to substitute

### 5. Competitive Rivalry
- Industry growth rate
- Number and balance of competitors
- Exit barriers
- Product differentiation
- Fixed cost / value added ratio

## Scoring
Each force scored: **Strong** (reduces attractiveness) / **Moderate** / **Weak** (increases attractiveness)

## When to Use
- Industry attractiveness assessment
- Market entry decisions
- Competitive positioning analysis
- **NOT** for resource-based strategy (use VRIO instead)

## HYPERION Application
The Strategy Analyst (Agent 14, STRONG tier) applies this framework when the question is about industry attractiveness or market entry. It does NOT apply it to every question — it selects the framework that illuminates the specific question.
